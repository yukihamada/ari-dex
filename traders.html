The Problem Today

You're Being Robbed Every Time You Trade

And you probably don't even know it.

What's actually happening?

When you submit a swap on a regular DEX, your order sits in a public waiting room (called a "mempool") before it gets processed. During that brief moment, automated bots can see exactly what you're about to do.

These bots jump in front of your trade, buy the token before you, push the price up, then sell right after you buy — pocketing the difference. This is called a sandwich attack.

Think of it like someone cutting in line at a store, buying up all the discounted items, then reselling them to you at a higher price.

How ARI Protects You

Your Trades Are Invisible to Bots

Three layers of protection, working together.

🔒

Your Order Is Encrypted

When you submit a trade on ARI, it's immediately encrypted. Nobody — not bots, not validators, not even us — can see your order details until it's been processed. Bots can't front-run what they can't see.

⚖

Fair Price for Everyone

ARI collects all orders within a 250-millisecond window and processes them as a batch. Every order in that batch gets the same fair price. No one can jump ahead of you because there's no "line" to cut.

80% MEV Returned to You

Even after all protections, if there's any remaining value that would normally go to bots (called MEV), ARI captures it and returns 80% directly back to you. You keep the money that used to be stolen.
